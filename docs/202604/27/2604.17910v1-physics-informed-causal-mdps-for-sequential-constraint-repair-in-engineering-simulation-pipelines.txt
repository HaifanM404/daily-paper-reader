## Abstract 

Off-policy learning in constrained MDPs with large binary state spaces faces a fundamental tension: causal identification of transition dynamics requires struc-tural assumptions, while sample-efficient policy learning requires state-space com-pression. We introduce PI-CMDP, a framework for CMDPs whose constraint dependencies form a layered DAG under a Lifecycle Ordering Assumption (LOA). We propose an Identify-Compress-Estimate pipeline: (i) Identify : LOA enables backdoor identification of causal edge weights for cross-layer pairs, with formal partial-identification bounds when LOA is violated; (ii) Compress : a Markov ab-straction compresses state cardinality from 2W L to (W + 1) L under layer-priority regularity and exchangeability; and (iii) Estimate : a physics-guided doubly-robust estimator remains unbiased and reduces the variance constant when the physics prior outperforms a learned model. We instantiate PI-CMDP on constraint repair in engineering simulation pipelines. On the TPS benchmark (4,206 episodes), PI-CMDP achieves 76.2% repair suc-cess rate with only 300 training episodes versus 70.8% for the strongest baseline (+5.4 pp), narrowing to +2.8 pp (83.4% vs. 80.6%) in the full-data regime, while substantially reducing cascade failure rates. All improvements are consistent across 5 independent seeds (paired t-test p < 0.02 ); we provide detailed effect sizes and caveats in Section 8. 

## 1 Introduction 

Off-policy learning in constrained MDPs faces a fundamental tension between identification and sample complexity. The state space of a CMDP with n binary constraint variables is exponential (2n), making both causal identification of transition dynamics and sample-efficient policy learning intractable in the general case. Existing approaches either assume fully known dynamics [ 5], re-quire mediator variables for front-door identification [ 7], or do not exploit structural properties of the constraint graph. A natural question arises: are there practically relevant classes of CMDPs where domain structure simultaneously enables causal identification and exponential state-space compression? 

We answer this affirmatively for CMDPs whose constraint dependencies form a layered DAG —a structure we call the Lifecycle Ordering Assumption (LOA). This structure arises naturally in engineering simulation pipelines (CFD, FEM, TPS), where con-straints are organized in strict layers (unit → physics → numerics → solver → execution) enforced by the build system. Violations cascade along directed dependencies, and repairing them in the wrong order triggers divergent loops. But the theoretical consequences of LOA extend beyond this application domain: any sequential repair or troubleshooting problem whose dependency graph admits a layered DAG topology can benefit from the identification and compression results we derive.

## 2 Background and Related Work 

Causal MDPs. Lu, Meisami, and Tewari [ 5 ] introduced Causal MDPs (C-MDPs) and proved a regret bound of O(HS √ZT ) where Z is a causal-graph-dependent measure exponentially smaller than |A| . Their analysis assumes the causal graph is known. We extend their framework to layered DAGs under LOA and LMC. The structural gain is primarily in state-space compression—from a worst-case bitmap-state cardinality 2W L to (W + 1) L. Liu et al. [ 4] reduce causal MDP planning to a sequence of fractional knapsack problems, achieving O(T log T ) planning complexity. Our topological pruning achieves a complementary reduction: branching factor from O(|A| ) to O(W )

per layer. 

Offline causal reinforcement learning. Namkoong et al. [ 7] use the front-door criterion with pessimism for offline causal RL. Our framework is complementary: we exploit the backdoor criterion where applicable (cross-layer pairs) and fall back to doubly-robust estimation for intra-layer pairs. We position LOA as a domain-grounded sufficient condition within recent graphical identification frameworks. 

Doubly-robust estimation. The DR/AIPW estimator [ ? ] and its √N -consistency are classical results [ 2]. We contribute a new analysis showing how incorporating a physics prior improves the convergence constant, analogous to the bias-variance trade-off in regularized estimation. 

Physics-informed machine learning & Troubleshooting. Physics-informed neural networks [ 8]incorporate PDE constraints as soft penalties. We use analytic physics solutions as a Bayesian prior on causal edge weights. For sequential troubleshooting, Heckerman et al. [ 3] formulate it as a decision problem under a belief network. CDG [ 1] is the direct predecessor of our work; we formally identify CDG as a depth-1 tree search under an observational approximation, and prove both the estimation bias and regret lower bound that motivate PI-CMDP. 

## 3 Problem Formulation 

3.1 Engineering Simulation Pipeline as a Layered DAG 

Let V = {v1, . . . , v n} be the set of constraints and E ⊆ V ×V the set of dependency edges. We define a layer function ℓ : V → { 1, . . . , L } corresponding to the pipeline stages (unit, physics, numerics, solver, execution). 

Assumption 1 (Lifecycle Ordering Assumption (LOA)) . For all edges (u, v ) ∈ E , if ℓ(u)̸ = ℓ(v),then ℓ(u) < ℓ (v), and there exists no directed path from v back to u. Formally, (V, E) is a DAG and for all (u, v ) ∈ E , ℓ(v) ≥ ℓ(u).

The maximum layer width is W = max ℓ |{ v : ℓ(v) = ℓ}| . Let Pa (v) denote the parents of node v in the CDG.

## 5 Compress: Compact Markov State Abstraction 

LOA enables a dramatic compression of the state space. Since n ≤ W L , the full bitmap-state cardinality is at most 2W L . We show that under the earliest-layer property and exchangeability, the count state ˜st = ( n(t), c ) with n(t) ∈ { 0, . . . , W }L is a Markov-sufficient statistic. 

Assumption 4 (Within-Layer Exchangeability) . For each layer ℓ, conditioned on the violation counts 

n(t) and context c, the expected repair outcome is the same for any violated constraint in layer ℓ.

Proposition 1 (Count State is Markov-Sufficient) . Under Assumption 4 and Condition 1, the aggregated process on count states ˜st = (n(t), c ) is Markov for the restricted policy class 

Π∗ = {π : π always acts on earliest layer }. The Bellman equation holds over the compact state space | ˜S| ≤ (W + 1) L.

## 7 Uncertainty-Aware Repair Planning 

Proposition 2 (Linear Step Suboptimality of Greedy CDG) . For every L ≥ 3, there exists a deterministic layered repair instance where a depth-1 greedy policy requires Ω( L) more repair steps than the optimal policy. 

Theorem 4 (Admissibility of Topological Pruning) . If the environment satisfies Monotone Layer-Priority Regularity (Condition 1, ρfeedback = 0 ), there exists an optimal policy whose action at every step lies entirely in the earliest violated layer A∗. This prunes the MCTS branching factor from 

O(|V| ) to O(W ).

## 8 Experiments 

8.1 Setup 

We evaluate on the TPS benchmark (4,206 episodes, L = 5 , W = 22 ). The 80/20 train/test split is stratified. All RSR values are mean ± SE over 5 independent seeds. 

Statistical methodology. All pairwise comparisons use a two-sided paired t-test across 5 seeds, reporting the t-statistic, degrees of freedom ( df = 4 ), mean paired difference ∆, and its standard error SE ∆. With only 5 seeds the statistical power is limited, and the reported p-values should be interpreted as indicative of effect direction and approximate magnitude rather than precise Type-I error control. The strong positive correlation of per-seed performance across methods ( r > 0.9)reduces paired-difference variance, which partly explains the moderate p-values despite limited replication. We encourage future work with larger seed budgets to sharpen these estimates.

8.6 Exchangeability Assumption Validation 

To validate the within-layer exchangeability assumption without hitting the sample complexity limits of Theorem 2, we constructed a mini-benchmark (L = 3 , W = 4 , 500 episodes) where the uncompressed state space is tractable. On this subset, comparing compact count-based PI-CMDP with a Full-Bitmap-PI-CMDP diagnostic variant shows less than 0.5% RSR gap ( 91 .2% vs 91 .5% ,

p = 0 .45 ), confirming exchangeability holds. However, when Full-Bitmap-PI-CMDP is deployed on the full benchmark (L = 5 , W = 22 ), its RSR collapses to 18.2% due to data sparsity in the 

2W L = 2 22 ×5 = 2 110 state space. This stark contrast corroborates the structural necessity of state compression proved in Theorem 2. 

## 9 Conclusion 

We presented PI-CMDP, a cohesive Identify-Compress-Estimate causal MDP framework for en-gineering pipelines. We provided formal partial-identification bounds for causal edge weights under LOA violations, established an information-theoretic state compression limit, and integrated a variance-reducing physics-guided AIPW estimator. Empirical results on TPS and CFD pipelines show consistent improvements across 5 independent seeds, particularly in data-scarce regimes and cascade failure risk reduction, though we note that the limited seed count warrants cautious interpretation of formal significance levels.
